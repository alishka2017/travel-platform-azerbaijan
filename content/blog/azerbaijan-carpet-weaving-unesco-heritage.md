In Azerbaijan, carpets are more than floor coverings. They are family heirlooms, storytelling devices, and repositories of cultural memory. The country’s carpet‑weaving tradition dates back at least 2,500 years, with patterns and techniques passed down through generations of women.

In 2010, Azerbaijani carpet weaving was inscribed on UNESCO’s Representative List of the Intangible Cultural Heritage of Humanity, recognizing its deep social and artistic significance.

## The Language of Symbols

Every element in an Azerbaijani carpet carries meaning. The patterns are a visual language that conveys wishes, protections, and stories.

- **Buta** (paisley): The most famous symbol, representing fire, fertility, and eternity. Often called the “flame of eternity.”
- **Göl** (medallion): Central motifs that symbolize unity, the sun, or a flower.
- **Kəpənək** (butterfly): Transformation, lightness, joy.
- **Su yolu** (water path): Wavy lines representing life‑giving water, prosperity.
- **Qoç buynuzu** (ram’s horn): Strength, masculinity, protection.
- **Elə‑bərdə** (hand‑over‑arm): Friendship, cooperation, marriage.

Colors also carry meaning: **red** for joy and protection, **blue** for sky and water, **yellow** for light and wealth, **green** for nature and Islam, **white** for purity and mourning.

## Regional Schools

Azerbaijan has seven distinct carpet‑weaving schools, each with its own style, palette, and techniques.

### 1. **Quba School** (Northeast)
Characterized by geometric precision and rich red backgrounds. Famous for **“Çiçi”** (geometric medallions) and **“Qımıl”** (small pattern) designs. Wool is dense, patterns intricate.

### 2. **Baku School** (Absheron)
Influenced by the city’s cosmopolitan history. Features floral motifs, pastel colors, and delicate compositions. Look for **“Xilə‑buta”** (paisley) and **“Qobustan”** (inspired by rock art).

### 3. **Şirvan School** (Central‑East)
Elegant, balanced designs with ivory or blue backgrounds. The **“Şirvan”** carpet is known for its central medallion surrounded by intricate borders.

### 4. **Qazax School** (West)
Bold geometric patterns in contrasting colors (red, blue, white). Often features large‑scale motifs like **“Qazax gölü”** (Kazakh medallion).

### 5. **Gəncə School** (West)
Similar to Qazax but with more floral elements and softer colors. Known for **“Gəncə gölü”** (Ganja medallion).

### 6. **Karabakh School** (Southwest)
The most colorful and diverse. Features fantastical animals, trees of life, and narrative scenes. **“Xanlıq”** (khanate) carpets are especially prized.

### 7. **Təbriz School** (Historically Azerbaijani, now in Iran)
Considered the pinnacle of artistic carpet weaving. Extremely fine knots, elaborate curvilinear designs, and masterful use of color. The **“Təbriz şah‑abbası”** (Shah Abbas pattern) is world‑famous.

## The Weaving Process

Traditional Azerbaijani carpets are knotted, not woven. The process is slow and meditative.

1. **Dyeing**: Natural dyes from plants, insects, and minerals: madder root (red), indigo (blue), walnut husks (brown), saffron (yellow).
2. **Warping**: Wool or cotton threads are stretched vertically on a loom (**dəzgah**).
3. **Knotting**: Using a knife (**xorcud**), the weaver ties thousands of symmetrical (**Gördes**) or asymmetrical (**Səneh**) knots per square decimeter.
4. **Finishing**: The pile is trimmed, the carpet is washed, and edges are bound.

A medium‑sized carpet (2×3m) can take 6–12 months to complete.

## Where to See & Buy

### Museums
- **Azerbaijan National Carpet Museum** (Baku): Shaped like a rolled carpet, this museum houses over 10,000 exhibits, including medieval fragments and contemporary works.
- **Quba Carpet Museum**: Focus on the northeastern school, with live demonstrations.
- **Şeki Caravan Palace**: Displays historical Karabakh carpets.

### Workshops & Markets
- **Baku’s Old City**: Several small workshops where you can watch weavers and buy directly.
- **Quba’s Carpet weaving centers**: **Qırmızı Qəsəbə** (Red Settlement) village is famous for its Jewish‑Azerbaijani carpet weavers.
- **Şeki’s Silk Carpets**: Visit the **Şeki xalça emalatxanası** for silk carpets with gold‑thread details.

## How to Choose an Authentic Carpet

1. **Check the knots**: Turn the carpet over. More knots per cm² means higher quality (good: 160,000–250,000 knots/m²).
2. **Natural materials**: Wool should be springy, silk should shimmer. Synthetic fibers feel plastic‑like.
3. **Natural dyes**: They have subtle variations; chemical dyes are uniform and brighter.
4. **Symmetry**: Patterns should be balanced, lines straight.
5. **Provenance**: Ask for the weaver’s name, village, and date. Reputable sellers provide certificates.

## The Future of the Craft

While traditional carpet‑weaving continues, contemporary artists like **Faig Ahmed** are pushing boundaries with surreal, deconstructed carpets that blend ancient techniques with modern art. His works have been exhibited at the Venice Biennale and the Louvre, bringing Azerbaijani carpets into the 21st century.

Owning an Azerbaijani carpet means owning a piece of living history – a tapestry of symbols, stories, and skilled hands that connect you to a millennia‑old tradition.
